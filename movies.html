Work as a movie director

Since stepping into the world of filmmaking in 2009, I've been on a mission to use the power of documentary storytelling to address pressing social issues. Over the past decade, I've strived to seamlessly blend artistry with advocacy, shining a light on the often-overlooked facets of our society.

Mivova yato (Women's Voices) 2022

Mivova yato (Women's Voices), is a poignant film from Gabon, courageously amplifies the silenced voices of women who have endured the harrowing experiences of sexual assault. Through intimate interviews and powerful narratives, the film provides a platform for these survivors to share their stories, fostering a sense of solidarity and empowerment within the community. Mivova yato (Women's Voices) not only sheds light on the pervasive issue of sexual violence but also serves as a catalyst for societal dialogue, challenging norms and advocating for a more compassionate and just future for women all over the world and especially from Gabon. The film's compassionate storytelling and commitment to breaking the silence make it a compelling testament to the strength and resilience of these courageous survivors.

No family 2013

In Gabon, "Without family" is the name given to the central prison of Libreville, because the inmates there are stigmatized and rejected from their family world. Knowing the importance of the family in Africa, this name clearly demonstrates the extent of the consequences of imprisonment on the social environment of an individual. Subject to different forms of stigmatization and exclusion, the inmates of our prisons (a situation almost identical to other African countries) therefore become "familyless", individuals for whom passage in the prison world carries many upheavals. By meeting Daniel, an ex-inmate, by discussing his post-prison itinerary and his experiences, we discover the repercussions of this "particular absence" that prison represents on the lives of prisoners in Gabon. We understand the desire for humanity of a man who, although released from prison, returns there to express his humanity and solidarity.

HANG ON!

Three women and a couple, all HIV-positive, decided to testify openly to denounce the stigma and help those hiding in their hands. Through their daily lives, their struggle in community life, their fight against the disease, these people show that it is possible to live with AIDS, if one accepts one's status by leading a responsible life

Elias

In a brief yet impactful narrative, the short film ELIAS confronts the stark reality of domestic violence, focusing on the lives of a women subjected to abuse by her husbands. Through evocative storytelling and raw performances, the film sheds light on the silent struggles of these women, urging society to acknowledge the pervasive issue of intimate partner violence. ELIAS serves as a powerful call to action, imploring viewers to break the cycle of abuse and foster a culture of empathy and support for those affected.

coordinator of the libreville documentary stopovers festival

As a dedicated filmmaker and visionary, I proudly take on the role of organizer for the 'Libreville Documentary Stopover,' a festival celebrating the power of non-fiction storytelling. Guided by my passion for impactful cinema, this festival aims to create a vibrant platform for filmmakers worldwide, fostering dialogue on pressing global issues. 'Libreville Documentary Stopover' is not just an event; it's a testament to the transformative potential of documentaries in shaping perspectives and inspiring change.
